Course Overview

Our Trail Riding Mastery course is designed for intermediate riders who want to take their mountain biking skills to the next level. This intensive 3-day program focuses on technical riding, managing challenging terrain, and improving your overall confidence on the trails.

Technical Skills Development

Learn advanced body positioning, cornering techniques, and obstacle navigation to handle challenging trail features with confidence.

Professional Coaching

Receive personalized instruction from our team of certified mountain bike coaches with racing and guiding experience.

Trail Selection Expertise

Discover how to read trails effectively and select optimal lines for smoother, faster, and safer riding on various terrains.

Alpine Trail Experience

Apply your new skills on world-class alpine trails in the breathtaking setting of Alpe d'Huez, with stunning views and varied terrain.

Course Details

What's Included

  • Professional instruction from certified coaches
  • Full suspension mountain bike rental (if required)
  • Safety equipment including helmet and protection pads
  • Lift passes for all three days
  • Daily lunch and refreshments
  • Course completion certificate
  • Photo package of your experience
  • Post-course skills development plan

Requirements

  • Intermediate mountain biking experience (minimum 1 season)
  • Ability to ride blue-graded trails comfortably
  • Good level of fitness (able to ride for 3-4 hours)
  • Minimum age: 16 years
  • Own appropriate clothing (riding shorts, gloves, etc.)

What to Bring

  • Weather-appropriate riding clothes
  • Riding gloves
  • Sunglasses and sunscreen
  • Small backpack
  • Personal water bottle
  • Camera (optional)
  • Preferred pedals (if using own bike)
  • Positive attitude!

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need to bring my own bike?

No, we provide high-quality full-suspension mountain bikes suitable for the course. However, if you prefer to use your own bike, you're welcome to bring it. We recommend a trail or enduro bike with at least 130mm of suspension travel.

What if the weather is bad?

Mountain weather can be unpredictable. Light rain doesn't stop us â€“ we'll provide riding tips for wet conditions. In case of severe weather, we'll modify the schedule or offer alternative activities. We never compromise on safety.

How fit do I need to be?

You should have a good level of fitness and be able to ride for 3-4 hours at a moderate pace. The focus is on skills rather than endurance, but better fitness means more enjoyment and faster progression.

What's the group size?

We maintain a maximum ratio of 6 students per instructor to ensure quality instruction and safety. For groups of 4 or more, we can arrange private courses on request.

Is travel insurance required?

Yes, we strongly recommend having appropriate travel and accident insurance that covers mountain biking activities. Many standard policies exclude "adventure sports," so check your coverage carefully.

Can I book extra coaching days?

Absolutely! Many participants choose to add private coaching sessions before or after the course. This is a great way to further refine specific skills or address personal challenges.
